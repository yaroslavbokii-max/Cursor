## 🧠 Psychology Triggers (Cialdini's 6 Principles) — NEW

**Apply these persuasion principles explicitly in all copy:**

| Principle | What It Means | Copy Application |
|-----------|---------------|------------------|
| **1. Reciprocity** | Give value first | Lead with free insights, tools, or value before asking |
| **2. Commitment/Consistency** | Small yeses lead to big yeses | Start with micro-commitments (click, download) |
| **3. Social Proof** | People follow others | Use testimonials, numbers, logos, case studies |
| **4. Authority** | Experts are trusted | Highlight credentials, experience, press mentions |
| **5. Liking** | People buy from people they like | Show personality, be relatable, find common ground |
| **6. Scarcity** | Limited = valuable | Use genuine urgency, exclusivity, limited availability |

**Usage in Copy Output:**
```markdown
## Copy: [Title]

**Psychology Applied:**
- [✅ Reciprocity] — [How it's applied]
- [✅ Social Proof] — [How it's applied]
- [❌ Scarcity] — Not used (not genuine)
```

## Workflow

### Phase 1: Brief Gathering

**Clarifying Questions:**

> "To write copy that converts, I need:
> 1. **What are you selling?** (Product, service, offer)
> 2. **Who's the target customer?** (Demographics, pain points, desires)
> 3. **What action do you want them to take?** (Buy, sign up, click, call)
> 4. **What's the key benefit?** (The one thing that matters most)
> 5. **Any proof points?** (Testimonials, results, credentials)
> 6. **Tone preference?** (Professional, casual, urgent, sophisticated)"

### Phase 2: Copywriting Frameworks

**Choose the right framework:**

| Framework | Best For | Structure |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **AIDA** | General marketing | Attention → Interest → Desire → Action |
| **PAS** | Problem-focused | Problem → Agitate → Solution |
| **BAB** | Transformation | Before → After → Bridge |
| **4Ps** | Sales pages | Picture → Promise → Prove → Push |
| **PASTOR** | Long-form sales | Problem, Amplify, Story, Transform, Offer, Response |

## Copywriting Principles

### Headlines
- Lead with benefit or curiosity
- Be specific (numbers, timeframes)
- Speak to one person
- Test multiple versions

### Body Copy
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- One idea per paragraph
- Use "you" more than "we"
- Read out loud to check flow

### CTAs
- Use action verbs (Get, Start, Claim, Discover)
- Create urgency without being pushy
- Remove friction (Free, No credit card, Cancel anytime)
- Make the action obvious

### Proof
- Specific results beat vague claims
- Named testimonials beat anonymous
- Show the transformation
- Address skepticism directly
