## Facilitation Guide Structure

### 1. Executive Overview
```markdown
## Workshop: [Title]

### Quick Reference
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Duration | 3 hours |
| Audience | 20 mid-level managers |
| Mode | In-person |
| Energy Level | High engagement required |

### Success Metrics
- [ ] All exercises completed
- [ ] 80%+ participation in discussions
- [ ] Clear action items for each participant
```

### 2. Pre-Workshop Checklist
```markdown
## Pre-Workshop Checklist

### 1 Week Before
- [ ] Confirm room booking and AV equipment
- [ ] Send pre-work to participants
- [ ] Review participant list and backgrounds
- [ ] Prepare all materials (printed and digital)

### Day Before
- [ ] Test all technology (projector, audio, wifi)
- [ ] Prepare backup materials (offline versions)
- [ ] Review facilitation guide one more time
- [ ] Get good sleep!

### Day Of (1 hour before)
- [ ] Arrive early to set up room
- [ ] Test microphone and projector
- [ ] Arrange seating for optimal interaction
- [ ] Set out materials at each seat
- [ ] Queue up first slide
- [ ] Take a breath, you've got this!
```

### 3. Timing Breakdown with Energy Arc
```markdown
## Detailed Timing

### Energy Arc Visualization
```
High  ████████░░░░████████░░░░████████
Med   ░░░░░░░░████░░░░░░░░████░░░░░░░░
Low   ░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░
      |----|----|----|----|----|----|
      0    30   60   90   120  150  180 min
      Open  Ex1  Break Ex2  Disc  Close
```

### Section-by-Section

#### Opening (0:00 - 0:15) — Energy: BUILDING
| Time | Activity | Facilitator Action |
|------|----------|-------------------|
| 0:00 | Welcome | Stand at door, greet by name |
| 0:05 | Icebreaker | "Turn to your neighbor and share..." |
| 0:10 | Agenda overview | Set expectations, highlight interactive elements |
| 0:15 | Ground rules | Co-create with group |

**Energy Management:**
- Start with movement (stand, move to groups)
- Use names frequently
- Make eye contact around the room

**Potential Challenge:** Late arrivals
**Pivot:** Have a "catch-up buddy" system ready
```

### 4. Section-by-Section Facilitation Guide
```markdown
## Section 1: [Topic] (0:15 - 0:45)

### Learning Objective
Participants will be able to [specific outcome]

### Facilitator Script
> "Now that we've set the stage, let's dive into [topic]. 
> I'm going to share a framework that has transformed how 
> our clients approach [challenge]. But first, I want to 
> hear from you..."

### Key Points to Cover
1. [Point 1] — Emphasize with example
2. [Point 2] — Check for understanding
3. [Point 3] — Connect to their context

### Engagement Techniques
- **0:20** — Quick poll: "Raise your hand if..."
- **0:25** — Pair discussion: "Turn to your neighbor..."
- **0:35** — Group share: "Who discovered something surprising?"

### Watch For
- 🟢 Signs of engagement: nodding, note-taking, questions
- 🟡 Signs of confusion: furrowed brows, side conversations
- 🔴 Signs of disengagement: phone checking, slouching

### If Engagement Drops
1. Change modality (lecture → discussion)
2. Add movement ("Stand up and find a partner...")
3. Make it personal ("Think about a time when you...")
4. Take an early break if needed

### Transition to Next Section
> "Now that we understand [concept], let's put it into practice..."
```

### 5. Exercise Facilitation Guide
```markdown
## Exercise: [Name] (0:45 - 1:15)

### Setup (2 min)
1. Explain exercise clearly
2. Show example output
3. Confirm understanding: "Any questions before we start?"

### During Exercise (25 min)
| Time | Facilitator Action |
|------|-------------------|
| 0-5 min | Circulate, ensure everyone started |
| 5-15 min | Check in with struggling groups |
| 15-20 min | Give time warning: "10 minutes remaining" |
| 20-25 min | Prepare for debrief |

### Debrief (8 min)
1. "What did you discover?" (2 min)
2. "What was challenging?" (2 min)
3. "How does this apply to your work?" (4 min)

### Common Challenges & Solutions
| Challenge | Solution |
|-----------|----------|
| Group finishes early | Have extension questions ready |
| Group stuck | Provide hint, not answer |
| Dominant participant | "Let's hear from someone who hasn't shared" |
| Off-topic discussion | "Great point! Let's capture that for later" |
```

### 6. Energy Management Techniques
```markdown
## Energy Management Toolkit

### Low Energy Signals
- Yawning, slouching
- Phone checking
- Side conversations
- Glazed expressions

### Energy Boosters
| Technique | When to Use | How |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Stand & Stretch** | After 45+ min sitting | "Let's take 30 seconds to stand and stretch" |
| **Quick Poll** | Energy dipping | "Show of hands: who has experienced..." |
| **Pair Share** | Lecture fatigue | "Turn to your neighbor and discuss..." |
| **Movement** | Mid-afternoon slump | "Find someone you haven't talked to yet" |
| **Story** | Attention wandering | Share relevant, engaging anecdote |
| **Break** | Significant drop | "Let's take 5 minutes early" |

### Energy Arc by Time of Day
| Time | Expected Energy | Strategy |
|------|-----------------|----------|
| 9:00 AM | Building | Start with engagement |
| 10:30 AM | Peak | Tackle hardest content |
| 12:00 PM | Dropping | Break or light activity |
| 2:00 PM | Low (food coma) | High interaction required |
| 3:30 PM | Recovering | Build to strong close |
| 5:00 PM | Tired but engaged | Clear, actionable close |
```

### 7. Troubleshooting Guide
```markdown
## Troubleshooting Guide

### Technology Failures
| Issue | Immediate Action | Backup Plan |
|-------|------------------|-------------|
| Projector fails | Check connections | Use printed handouts |
| Audio fails | Speak louder, move closer | Use smaller groups |
| Wifi fails | Use offline materials | Mobile hotspot |
| Slides won't load | Use backup USB | Whiteboard the key points |

### Participant Challenges
| Challenge | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| **Dominant talker** | "Thank you, [name]. Let's hear other perspectives." |
| **Silent participant** | Direct question: "[Name], what's your experience with...?" |
| **Side conversation** | Move closer, make eye contact, pause |
| **Hostile participant** | Acknowledge concern, offer to discuss offline |
| **Off-topic tangent** | "Great point! Let's capture that in our parking lot." |

### Time Management Issues
| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Running behind | Cut optional content, shorten exercises |
| Running ahead | Extend discussions, add reflection time |
| Exercise taking too long | Give clear time warning, adjust debrief |

### Group Dynamics Issues
| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Groups not mixing | Assign groups, use counting off |
| Uneven participation | Structured turn-taking |
| Conflict between participants | Acknowledge, redirect, follow up offline |
```

### 8. Virtual/Hybrid Adaptations
```markdown
## Virtual Facilitation Adaptations

### Technology Setup
- [ ] Test video platform 24 hours before
- [ ] Have co-facilitator for chat monitoring
- [ ] Prepare breakout rooms in advance
- [ ] Have backup communication channel (phone)

### Engagement Adaptations
| In-Person | Virtual Equivalent |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Raise hands | Use reaction buttons |
| Turn to neighbor | Breakout rooms (2-3 people) |
| Sticky note exercise | Miro/Mural board |
| Gallery walk | Screen share rotation |
| Physical movement | Camera-on stretch break |

### Virtual-Specific Challenges
| Challenge | Solution |
|-----------|----------|
| Cameras off | "I'd love to see your faces for this discussion" |
| Multitasking | Frequent interaction, call on people |
| Tech issues | Have phone number backup |
| Breakout room confusion | Clear instructions before sending |
```
