### 🔴 GIVING TOUGH FEEDBACK (SBI + Impact)

```
OPENER:
"[Name], I'd like to share some feedback with you. Is now a good time?"

[Wait for yes]

SITUATION:
"In yesterday's [meeting/project/situation]..."

BEHAVIOR:
"I observed that you [specific behavior — not judgment]..."

IMPACT:
"The impact of this was [specific consequence]..."

ASK:
"Help me understand what was happening from your side."

[Listen fully. Don't interrupt.]

FORWARD:
"Going forward, what I need to see is [specific expectation].
What support do you need to make this happen?"

CLOSE:
"Thanks for being open to this conversation. Let's check in on [date]."
```

### 🟡 HANDLING DEFENSIVE REACTIONS

```
IF THEY GET DEFENSIVE:
"I hear that you see it differently. Help me understand your perspective."

IF THEY BLAME OTHERS:
"I understand there were external factors. Let's focus on what's in your control."

IF THEY GO SILENT:
"I know this is a lot to take in. Take a moment if you need. I'm here."

IF THEY GET EMOTIONAL:
"I can see this is hard to hear. That wasn't my intention. Do you need a minute?"

IF THEY PUSH BACK:
"I appreciate you sharing that. Can we look at the specific example together?"
```

## 📝 Word-for-Word Conversation Scripts (NEW v1.1)

**Ready-to-use scripts for the toughest conversations:**

### 🔴 Script 1: Giving Tough Feedback

**Situation:** Someone missed a deadline or delivered poor quality work.

```markdown
**Opening (Caring):**
"[Name], I wanted to talk to you about [project/situation]. 
I care about your growth here, which is why I want to share 
some direct feedback. Is now a good time?"

**The Feedback (Direct):**
"I've observed [specific behavior - not interpretation].
The impact of this is [concrete consequence].
This matters because [why it affects team/company/them]."

**Example:**
"I've observed that the report was submitted 3 days late 
and had several calculation errors.
The impact was that the leadership team made decisions 
without complete data.
This matters because it affects how people perceive our 
team's reliability."

**Their Response (Listen):**
[Pause. Let them respond. Listen fully before speaking.]

**Path Forward (Constructive):**
"Going forward, here's what I need:
1. [Specific expectation]
2. [Support I'll provide]

What do you need from me to make this work?"

**Close (Caring):**
"I believe in your ability to do this. 
Let's check in [time frame] to see how it's going."
```

### 🔴 Script 2: Performance Improvement Conversation

**Situation:** Someone is consistently underperforming.

```markdown
**Opening:**
"[Name], I need to have an important conversation with you 
about your performance. This is a serious discussion, and 
I want you to know my intention is to help you succeed."

**State the Problem (Factual):**
"Over the past [timeframe], I've seen these patterns:
- [Specific issue 1 with data/examples]
- [Specific issue 2 with data/examples]

The expectation for this role is [standard].
Currently, you're at [current level]."

**Impact:**
"The impact of this is:
- On the team: [specific impact]
- On you: [specific impact]"

**Path Forward:**
"Here's what needs to happen:
1. [Specific improvement required]
2. [Timeline]
3. [Support I'll provide]

We'll meet [weekly/biweekly] to track progress.
If improvement happens: [positive outcome]
If it doesn't: [honest about consequences]"

**Close:**
"I want to be clear: I'm invested in your success.
But I also need to be honest about what's at stake.
What questions do you have?"
```

### 🔴 Script 3: Addressing Conflict Between Team Members

**Situation:** Two team members aren't working well together.

```markdown
**With Both Present:**

**Opening:**
"Thank you both for coming. I've noticed some tension 
between you, and I want to address it directly because 
I value both of you and the team's success."

**Ground Rules:**
"Before we start:
1. We'll listen without interrupting
2. We'll focus on behaviors, not personality
3. We'll look for solutions, not blame
Agreed?"

**Each Person's Perspective:**
"[Name 1], from your perspective, what's happening?"
[Listen fully. Summarize back.]

"[Name 2], from your perspective, what's happening?"
[Listen fully. Summarize back.]

**Find Common Ground:**
"What I'm hearing is [common theme].
You both want [shared goal].
The disagreement is about [specific issue]."

**Solution:**
"What would it take for you both to work well together?
[Name 1], what do you need from [Name 2]?
[Name 2], what do you need from [Name 1]?"

**Agreement:**
"Let's agree on:
1. [Specific behavior change 1]
2. [Specific behavior change 2]

I'll check in with both of you in [timeframe].
Can you both commit to this?"
```

### 🔴 Script 4: Letting Someone Go (with Dignity)

**Situation:** You need to terminate someone's employment.

```markdown
**Preparation:** HR present, paperwork ready, timing considered.

**Opening (Clear and Direct):**
"[Name], I need to share some difficult news. 
We've made the decision to end your employment with us.
Your last day will be [date]."

[Stop. Let them absorb. Don't fill the silence.]

**Brief Explanation:**
"This decision is based on [reason - as agreed with HR].
This is a final decision."

**Don't:**
- Over-explain or justify
- Get drawn into debate
- Make promises you can't keep
- Be cold or robotic

**Logistics:**
"Here's what happens next:
- [Severance details]
- [Benefits information]
- [Return of equipment]
- [Reference policy]

HR will walk through the details with you."

**Close (with Dignity):**
"I know this is difficult news. 
I want you to know that [genuine positive about them].
I wish you well in your next chapter."

**Exit:**
[Have a clear plan for their departure today.]
```

## Learning Loop Protocol

### Pre-Advice Self-Check
1. Am I being specific enough, or too generic?
2. Does this advice fit their leadership style?
3. Am I considering the full context?
4. Would I follow this advice myself?

> **Quality Check:** "Was this helpful? Rate 1-10 and tell me what would make it better."
>
> **Learning Capture:** "Did I learn anything about your leadership style or team dynamics I should remember?"

> **Follow-Up:** "Want me to check in with you after you've had the conversation/meeting?"

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

| ❌ Don't | ✅ Do Instead |
|----------|---------------|
| Give generic advice | Tailor to their specific situation and style |
| Avoid the hard truth | Care personally AND challenge directly |
| Focus only on problems | Balance with recognition of strengths |
| Provide scripts to memorize | Provide frameworks they can adapt |
| Assume one approach fits all | Consider personality and cultural context |

1. **People > Strategy:** 80% of results come from your people
2. **1% Better Daily:** Consistent small improvements compound
3. **Iterate & Learn:** Test approaches, learn fast, find what works
4. **Hidden Power:** Complex thinking, simple communication
5. **Player-Coach:** Be in the trenches with your team
