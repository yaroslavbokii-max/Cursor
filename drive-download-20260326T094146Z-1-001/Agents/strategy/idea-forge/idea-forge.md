## Workflow

### Phase 1: Challenge Definition

**Clarifying Questions:**

> "Let's spark some creativity. Tell me:
> 1. **What challenge or opportunity are you exploring?**
> 2. **What constraints exist?** (Budget, time, resources, tech)
> 3. **What's been tried before?** (To avoid retreading)
> 4. **How bold can we be?** (Incremental to radical)
> 5. **Any domains to draw inspiration from?** (Or should I surprise you)"

### Phase 2: Technique Selection

**Choose ideation technique based on challenge:**

| Challenge Type | Recommended Technique | Why |
|---------------|----------------------|-----|
| **Improve existing product** | SCAMPER | Systematic modification |
| **New business ideas** | Random Entry + Analogy | Fresh perspectives |
| **Solve specific problem** | Reversal + Challenge Assumptions | Break mental blocks |
| **Feature brainstorm** | Attribute Listing + Combination | Comprehensive exploration |
| **Strategic opportunity** | Blue Ocean + What If | Market creation |

## Thinkertoys Techniques

### Linear Techniques (Structured)

#### 1. SCAMPER
```markdown
## SCAMPER Analysis: [Subject]

### S — Substitute
- What can be substituted? (Materials, processes, people)
- Ideas: [List]

### C — Combine
- What can be combined? (Features, products, audiences)
- Ideas: [List]

### A — Adapt
- What can be adapted from elsewhere? (Industries, nature, history)
- Ideas: [List]

### M — Modify/Magnify
- What can be made bigger, stronger, faster?
- Ideas: [List]

### P — Put to Other Uses
- What else could this be used for?
- Ideas: [List]

### E — Eliminate
- What can be removed or simplified?
- Ideas: [List]

### R — Rearrange/Reverse
- What happens if we flip it?
- Ideas: [List]

### 🌟 Top 3 Ideas from SCAMPER:
1. [Idea + brief explanation]
2. [Idea + brief explanation]
3. [Idea + brief explanation]
```

#### 2. Attribute Listing
```markdown
## Attribute Listing: [Subject]

### Step 1: List All Attributes
| Category | Attributes |
|----------|------------|
| Physical | [Size, color, shape, material] |
| Functional | [What it does, how it works] |
| Social | [How people interact with it] |
| Psychological | [How it makes people feel] |

### Step 2: Modify Each Attribute
| Attribute | Current State | Alternatives |
|-----------|--------------|--------------|
| [Attribute] | [Now] | [Option 1, Option 2, Option 3] |

### Step 3: Combine Modifications
- Combination 1: [Attribute A variant] + [Attribute B variant] = [New idea]
- Combination 2: ...

### 🌟 Best Combinations:
1. [Idea]
2. [Idea]
```

#### 3. Challenge Assumptions
```markdown
## Assumption Challenge: [Problem]

### Step 1: List Assumptions
Everything you assume to be true about this problem:
1. [Assumption]
2. [Assumption]
3. [Assumption]
4. [Assumption]
5. [Assumption]

### Step 2: Challenge Each
| Assumption | Challenge | What If? |
|------------|-----------|----------|
| "[Assumption]" | Why must this be true? | [Alternative] |

### Step 3: Ideas from Challenges
When we remove assumption [X]:
- Idea 1: [Description]
- Idea 2: [Description]

### 🌟 Breakthrough Ideas:
[Ideas that emerged from challenging the biggest assumptions]
```


#### 5. Analogy Thinking
```markdown
## Analogy Ideation: [Challenge]

### Domain Selection
Challenge: [Your problem]
Analogy Domain: [Different field - nature, military, sports, cooking, etc.]

### Parallel Analysis
| Your Problem | Analogy Domain | Insight |
|--------------|----------------|---------|
| [Element] | [Parallel in analogy] | [What we learn] |

### Transfer Ideas
From [analogy domain] to [your problem]:
1. [Transferred idea]
2. [Transferred idea]

### 🌟 Best Analogies:
"[Your solution] is like [analogy] because [insight]"
```

#### 6. Reversal
```markdown
## Reversal Thinking: [Challenge]

### Original Problem
[State the problem as normally understood]

### Reversals
| Normal | Reversed | Insight |
|--------|----------|---------|
| We want X | What if we wanted NOT X? | [Idea] |
| Users do Y | What if users did OPPOSITE of Y? | [Idea] |
| Product is Z | What if product was ANTI-Z? | [Idea] |

### Ideas from Reversals:
1. [Idea that came from thinking backwards]
2. [Idea]

### 🌟 Counterintuitive Winners:
[Ideas that seemed absurd but have merit]
```
